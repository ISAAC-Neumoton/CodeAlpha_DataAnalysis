## What is Olist?

**Olist** is a Brazilian technology company that operates a marketplace platform connecting small and medium-sized sellers across Brazil to large e-commerce channels (like Amazon, Magazine Luiza, etc.).  

The **Olist Brazilian E-commerce Dataset** is a public, anonymized dataset released by Olist on Kaggle. It contains **real transactional data** from 2016 to 2018, including:

- ~100,000 orders
- 9 interconnected CSV files: customers, sellers, products, orders, payments, reviews, geolocation, and translations
- Rich details: product attributes (weight, dimensions, category), customer review scores + full text, payment methods, shipping timelines, and geographic coordinates.

This dataset is widely used for learning and practicing end-to-end data analytics, ETL, sentiment analysis, and predictive modeling because it mimics a real e-commerce ecosystem with complex relational structures.

## Problem Statement

E‑commerce platforms and sellers lack clarity on which factors most consistently predict product success. Is lowering price always effective? Do star ratings or written reviews matter more? Does free shipping justify its cost? How much does seller location influence sales? Without answers, marketing budgets and operational decisions are often misallocated.

## Project Objective

Determine how **product attributes, customer feedback, and geographic factors** influence product sales – and identify the strongest predictors of **high‑performing products** in an e‑commerce environment.

## Key Research Questions

1. How does product price affect sales volume, and what is the optimal price range for high‑performing products?

2. Do customer review scores (1–5 stars) and written review sentiment independently predict product success, or does one dominate?

3. What is the impact of shipping cost (as a percentage of product price) on conversion and sales – and at what threshold do customers abandon?

4. How does seller location relative to the customer’s region influence delivery speed, review scores, and repeat purchase likelihood?

5. Which product categories respond most strongly to low shipping costs versus high review scores – and how should category‑specific strategies differ?

6. Can natural language processing (NLP) of review text uncover hidden drivers of product failure (e.g., delivery complaints, quality issues) that star ratings miss?

7. How does delivery delay (actual vs. estimated delivery date) affect customer satisfaction, review scores, and the probability of future orders from the same customer?

## Scope

- Load and clean 9 related tables
- Perform exploratory and diagnostic analysis
- Create data visualizations and interactive dashboards
- Apply sentiment analysis (NLP) on customer reviews
- Build predictive models to identify key success factors
- Deliver prescriptive recommendations for sellers and platform managers
